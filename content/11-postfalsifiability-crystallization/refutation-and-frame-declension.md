# Refutation and Frame Declension

## Definition

Two attacks can both end with a claim rejected, yet differ in what they touched. **Refutation** defeats a claim *under its own conditions*: it accepts the vocabulary, the setpoints, the costs, and the purposes that make the claim available, and shows that within those the claim fails — a same-level counter-instance, an internal contradiction, a binding whose actuator does not reduce its named error, a coupled controller that does not survive its own removal test. **Frame declension** does not defeat the claim; it *refuses the conditions* under which the claim would be assessable at all — the terms, the aims, the price of admission. The first is immanent: it lands inside. The second is transcendent to the case: it steps out rather than losing within.

Both are legitimate. The two-axis ledger already fixes this — a `[[frame-internal-tier|frame-internal (FT)]]` claim follows from accepted definitions, so the *only* attack on it is to decline the frame; a `[[carving-tier|carving (CV)]]` claim invites counter-instance from within. What this page adds is that the two modes are **non-fungible**: neither may be performed under the name of the other, and declension — though permitted — is never free.

## Type and formal status

> **Refutation / frame declension. E:** Derived, **CV** — a two-way *carving* of how attacks land, contestable by counter-instance (a third irreducible mode of attack) or by a better carving (a boundary criterion that supersedes this one). It introduces no forced or exempt tier; the distinction is itself Derived and marked, and answers to the same standard it describes. **A:** the carving *aspires* to track a real joint — the difference between a frame that lost and a frame that was left is a difference in the world of the dispute, not merely in our description of it; the aspiration is inaccurate if that joint proves undecidable where it is load-bearing. This is a **treatise-side extension, held contestable**, downstream of `[[falsification-standard|the falsification standard]]`. It is not canonical and claims no authority beyond the discriminating work it does.

## What it regulates

It regulates the **flattening of the attack surface into a single kind**. Two collapses threaten `[[falsification-standard|the standard]]`:

- If every rejection is scored as internal *refutation*, then declining the frame is treated as illegitimate or as unintelligible noise. The frame becomes the only sayable frame; an objection must be spoken in its cases or dismissed. That is `[[absolutization|absolutization]]` of the vocabulary — the frame exempting itself from the possibility of being *left*.
- If every rejection is scored as *declension*, then no claim can be defeated on its own terms; any same-level contradiction is rerouted as "you simply do not accept our terms." The frame is sealed, the `[[self-sealing-test|self-sealing test]]` fails, and the maintainer has installed the shield the anti-evasion rule forbids.

The excesses, and the channel each is regulated through:

```text
e_R = totalization   (only-refutation: the frame becomes the only permissible vocabulary)
e_D = self-sealing   (only-declension: every internal defeat is rerouted as refusal)

e_R -> declension    the standing possibility of declining the frame keeps it non-total
e_D -> refutation    a same-conditions defeat cannot be declined away
```

The errors are not the two modes themselves; they are the excesses each mode develops *in isolation*. So the page supplies exactly the discrimination `[[falsification-standard|the falsification standard]]` demands — that declension not be misreported as internal refutation, and that refutation not be dodged as declension — and it marks the seam between `[[frame-internal-tier|FT]]` and `[[carving-tier|CV]]` claims where the two modes divide.

## What regulates it

- `[[same-level-attack-rule|The same-level attack rule]]` fixes what counts as a genuine internal refutation — right type, right abstraction level. Without it, "refutation" is unbounded and any mismatch could pose as an internal defeat.
- The **anti-evasion test**, a sub-rule of the `[[self-sealing-test|self-sealing test]]`, forbids converting a same-level contradiction into declension, and forbids smuggling a contradiction in under the banner of principled refusal. It runs in both directions.
- The **cost ledger**: a decliner must state which explanatory and operational capacities are surrendered or replaced. Because `[[refusal-as-participation|refusal is itself participation]]` — it selects, routes, signifies, and leaves a consequence — a declension is not an exit from assessment; it can be priced.
- `[[causal-falsifiability|Causal falsifiability]]`: a declension logged but producing no change in what is done is ornamental; a refutation that alters nothing is a logged failed attack, not a defeat.
- Its own **CV mark**: the two-way carving is contestable. A demonstrated third mode, or an undecidable boundary, revises it.

## Valid attack surface

Attack the distinction at its own level, not by anecdote. A valid attack either (1) **exhibits a third irreducible mode** — an attack that is neither a same-conditions defeat nor a refusal-of-conditions, yet is a real attack the binary cannot host (a partial-frame or oblique-frame attack that the FT/CV split genuinely cannot represent); or (2) **shows the boundary is undecidable in a load-bearing case** — that no criterion separates "lost inside the frame" from "declined the frame" exactly where the discrimination is needed, so the distinction does no regulatory work. Contesting the *labels* while granting the two operations is not an attack on the claim; it is a better-carving proposal, which the CV mark already invites — see `[[attack-type-matching|attack-type matching]]`.

## What happens if isolated

The two isolation failures are the error channels made concrete.

- **Refutation without declension → totalization.** If the frame cannot be *declined*, only defeated from inside, it silently becomes the horizon of all permissible speech; every rival must translate into its cases before it may object, and untranslatable objections vanish. This is closure with no outside — the frame claiming to be the whole of meaning rather than one carving within it.
- **Declension without refutation → self-sealing.** If nothing can be defeated on its own terms, every internal contradiction is answered by "you decline our frame," and the frame is immunized against its own marks. This is the `[[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]]` at the level of method: the frame promoted beyond attack, exempt while everything else stays contestable.

Each isolation manufactures precisely the excess the other mode checks — which is why they are not two separable tools but a cross-regulated pair, a local instance (signpost, not identity) of the `[[reciprocal-attack-surfaces|reciprocal-attack-surfaces]]` pattern and, above it, of `[[no-escape-no-exemption|no escape, no exemption]]`.

## What larger property emerges from the coupling

Held together, the pair yields **honest, typed, costed contestability**: an attack surface that is neither a fortress (only-internal-refutation, no exit) nor a frictionless dodge (everything is declension). At any resolution one can say *which* happened — the frame lost on its own terms, or the frame was left for another vocabulary — and, if it was left, *at what price*. This is the discrimination `[[falsification-standard|the falsification standard]]` presupposes and the condition under which `[[postfalsifiability|postfalsifiability]]` stays honest: a formulation that dies to a same-conditions defeat is a genuine `[[outcomes-of-attack|deletion or revision]]`, whereas a vocabulary merely abandoned is a change of subject, not a kill — and neither may be booked as the other. Residue: the pair presumes rival frames share *some* consequence on which cost is legible. Where two frames are fully incommensurable, the price of declension becomes unstatable and the coupling thins toward the kill condition below.

## What would actually kill the claim

The distinction dies if either holds:

1. **The shield operates without cost.** A maintainer repeatedly reroutes a genuine same-level internal contradiction into "you decline our frame," and the system records no surrendered capacity and changes nothing. Then the anti-evasion test is inert, declension is doing refutation's dodging for free, and the two modes are not in fact kept distinct — the page describes a discipline it does not enforce.
2. **No shared consequence exists.** If rival frames share no consequence whatsoever, declension can never be priced and cross-frame comparison is impossible; "cost of declining" is undefined, and from outside there is no way to tell a frame that lost from a frame that was left. The joint the carving claims to track is then simply not there.

Either is a same-level defeat and would force deletion or a re-carving, not a footnote. A *failed* attempt to exhibit a third mode is logged as a failed attack; it is never counted as confirmation that the binary is complete.

## Prohibited misreadings

- **Declension is not defeat of the objector, and not illegitimate.** Declining the frame is a permitted move — the only available attack on an `[[frame-internal-tier|FT]]` claim. Treating a decliner as having "conceded" inverts the ledger.
- **Declension is not free.** It surrenders or replaces explanatory and operational capacities; the decliner owes the cost ledger. "I reject your terms" without an accounting is an assertion, not yet an attack.
- **Do not use declension as a shield.** Answering a same-level internal contradiction with "you merely decline our frame" is the anti-evasion violation — a `[[self-sealing-test|self-sealing]]` failure and, sustained, the `[[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]]`. The reverse is equally barred: a genuine internal contradiction may not be re-dressed as principled refusal.
- **A failed refutation is not confirmation.** A same-conditions attack that does not land is a logged failed attack, never evidence that the claim is true or that the frame is complete.
- **Refutation and declension are not interchangeable.** They touch different things and carry different bills; scoring one as the other is a category error that corrupts every downstream `[[outcomes-of-attack|outcome]]`.
- **This distinction is not itself frame-exempt.** It is `[[derived|Derived]]`, CV. A third mode of attack or an undecidable boundary revises it; it claims no exemption it denies to others.
