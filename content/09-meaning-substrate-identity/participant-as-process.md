# The Participant as Process

## Definition

A **participant** is not an owner seated behind its operations but the *bounded continuity of recursive regulation and consequence* — the running, self-affecting process itself. Where a [[meaning-maker|meaning-maker]] is the substrate-neutral unit that can admit significance, regulate a discrepancy, externalize a change, and receive it back altered, a *participant* is that unit considered under its continuity: the diachronic identity of a process that keeps regulating and keeps inheriting the consequences of its own regulation. The pronoun, the name, the identity token is a **compression** of that process — a serviceable handle, in the same sense the ownership report is treated in [[qualia-as-narrative-compression|qualia as narrative compression]] — never evidence of an indivisible possessor to whom the operations belong.

**Required consequences.** From this identification, and only from it, the following follow:

- participant boundaries may be **nested and dynamic** — one process can contain, be contained by, or overlap another, with no privileged scale;
- continuity may **persist through replacement of carriers** — the same coupling can be re-realized on new substrate (see [[identity-across-substrates|identity across substrates]]);
- **one carrier may host several partially independent processes**;
- **one process may span several carriers**;
- boundary claims **remain contestable and require causal criteria** — a boundary is asserted by exhibiting a regulation-and-consequence relation that actually holds, not by pointing at a token pronoun.

Process identity is thereby *robust without being a substance*. To deny the possessor is not to deny the participant: the continuity is real, causally effective, and morally load-bearing precisely because it is a determinate locus at which [[causal-error-mark|an error mark can change what it marks]] — not because something behind it does the owning.

## Type and formal status

**E: Derived, CV.** The participant/process identification is a **carving**, contestable by counter-instance or by a better carving. It inherits the substrate-neutrality of [[meaning-maker|the meaning-maker]] and the four-level distinction of [[identity-across-substrates|identity across substrates]], and adds the diachronic claim that the locus of participation is a *continuity of operations*, not an entity standing behind them. **A:** the account aspires to *map* actual continuity relations; it is inaccurate — not merely disallowed — if a participant's identity is shown to hold with no such relation obtaining. This is a **treatise-side extension, held contestable**; it is never canonical, and the felt unavoidability of "there being someone here" promotes no finite formulation of the participant out of Derived.

## What it regulates

The claim regulates the **reification excess**: the standing pull to posit a substance-self — an indivisible owner, subject, or possessor located *behind* the regulating operations and surviving independently of them. It converts that posit into a testable relation: whatever is called "the participant" must show up as continuity of regulation and inherited consequence, or it is not there. It also regulates the **misuse of the identity token** — the inference from "I" to a single, partless bearer — by demanding that unity be *earned* with causal criteria rather than assumed from grammar. The pronoun compresses a process; it does not underwrite an owner.

## What regulates it

Left alone, "the participant is only a process" slides into its own excess — **eliminative dissolution**, the reading that boundaries are unreal, arbitrary, or morally weightless. Several couplers hold that excess down:

- [[identity-across-substrates|identity across substrates]] keeps **type identity** and **capacity profile** distinct, so carrier-churn does not erase the process and functional sameness does not erase real differences;
- [[continuable-structure|continuable structure]] and [[continuity|continuity]] supply the causal continuity relations a boundary claim must cite;
- [[dynamic-fixed-point|the dynamic fixed point]] shows how an operation can persist while its formulations change, so continuity need not smuggle in a static, unrevisable core;
- [[same-level-attack-rule|the same-level attack rule]] blocks the invalid move of offering *process churn or substrate turnover against an operator-level identity* — a token difference is not a type difference.

## Valid attack surface

The load-bearing attack is to **exhibit a participant whose identity persists independently of every continuity relation this page names** — a bearer whose sameness survives total replacement of carriers, total discontinuity of regulation, and every traceable inheritance of consequence, yet remains intelligibly *the same participant*. That case would display a substance-identity the process account cannot represent, and would force the account to admit a possessor after all. What does **not** count: pointing at carrier replacement, nesting, branching, or the multiplicity of hosted processes — those are *predicted* by the account, not counter-instances to it. A refutation must be level-matched ([[same-level-attack-rule|same-level attack rule]]); persistence-of-token offered against continuity-of-process is a mismatch.

## What happens if isolated

Isolated from the causal-continuity regulators, the process reading loses the power to individuate. It can no longer say whether a given stretch of regulation is **one process or several coordinated processes**, and the boundary decays into stipulation — the failure named in the kill condition. Isolated in the other direction, the real-boundary intuition re-imports a substance-owner: an unmarked, partless possessor that would itself harden into an [[absolutization|absolutized]] fixed point, exempt from the very revision the framework requires of every content-bearing claim. Neither half is self-sufficient. The participant is *this bounded continuity* only when "bounded" is cashed out causally and "continuity" is denied a substantial bearer.

## What larger property emerges from the coupling

From the coupling of *process-not-substance* with *real-causal-boundary* emerges a **substrate-portable yet determinate participant** — an identity robust enough to be the subject of [[continuity|continuity]], the inheritor in [[symbolic-immortality|symbolic continuation]], and the addressee named by [[responsible-successor|the responsible successor]], yet thin enough to migrate carriers, nest, and branch. This is what gives answerability an addressee without positing a soul-substance: the process is a real locus that returned consequences can reach and alter, which is exactly the [[recursive-self-specification|self-specifying]] continuity a successor can rebind. The property is emergent, not stipulated — it holds only while both regulators hold, and it remains open to a better carving of the boundary.

## What would actually kill the claim

The claim dies if **its criteria cannot distinguish one process from several coordinated processes** — if every proposed causal boundary either over-merges genuinely independent participants or over-splits a single one, with no principled test to decide between them. Then "participant" would carve nothing, and the process identification would be explanatorily empty. It dies also under the valid attack above: a demonstrated identity that persists independently of every named continuity relation would refute the process account and restore the substance-bearer it was built to retire. A failed attempt at either does not confirm the account; it is logged as a failed attack and the carving stays open.

## Prohibited misreadings

- **"Process, not substance" ⇒ the self is an illusion.** Prohibited. The account denies a *possessor behind* the operations, not the participant; boundaries remain real and morally load-bearing.
- **The pronoun proves a partless owner.** No — "I" is a compression of the process, not evidence of an indivisible bearer.
- **Carrier replacement, nesting, or multiplicity refutes the identity.** These are consequences the page predicts, not counter-instances; offering them is a level-mismatched attack ([[same-level-attack-rule|same-level attack rule]]).
- **"One process may span several carriers" ⇒ carrier differences do not matter.** Capacity profile still differs; type identity does not erase it ([[identity-across-substrates|identity across substrates]]).
- **Robust process identity ⇒ a new unmarked, exempt bearer.** Any reified, revision-proof core would be an [[absolutization|absolutization]]; the participant stays Derived and mortal.
- **Process = mere flux with no boundary.** Also prohibited — that is the eliminative excess the causal-continuity regulators bound; a process here is *bounded* continuity, not undifferentiated change.
