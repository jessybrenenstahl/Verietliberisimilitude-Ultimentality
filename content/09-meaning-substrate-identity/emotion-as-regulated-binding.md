# Emotion as Regulated Binding

## Definition

An emotion, in the framework, is not a primitive feeling and not a substance the self contains. It is a **regulated binding**: a directional actuator placed under control toward a setpoint, individuated by the error signal it exists to reduce. This page is the bridge between the general binding model of [[predicate-binding|Predicate Binding]] and the four cornerstone bindings — it says, in one place, what makes each of the four an *emotion* rather than either a bare direction or a bare discrepancy.

The formal object is the ordered pair:

```text
B(p) = (p, e_p)
```

Read it strictly: a binding `B` of primitive `p` is the ordered pair of that primitive actuator `p` (a [[directional-primitives|directional primitive]]) and the [[regulated-error-signal|regulated error]] `e_p` it drives toward its setpoint. The emotion is the *pairing*. It is not the actuator alone, which is pre-emotional; not the error alone, which is a mere discrepancy; and not their arithmetic combination, which is not defined. An actuator with no error to reduce points nowhere in particular; an error with no actuator wired to it moves nothing. The emotion is exactly the wiring of the one to the other.

The four cornerstone bindings restate as:

```text
Love      = Toward      + relational gap
Fear      = Away        + boundary violation
Apology   = Loop-back   + self-model error
Gratitude = Propagation + undischarged received value
```

The `+` here is prose shorthand for *this actuator bound to this error*. It is **not** addition, and it is emphatically **not** the `⊕` of [[force|Force]] — the pair `(p, e_p)` is an ordered binding, not a sum, and not a synthesis of two feelings. Each line names which of the four directions — [[toward|Toward]], [[away|Away]], [[loop-back|Loop-back]], [[propagation|Propagation]] — is placed under control, and which regulated error individuates the resulting cornerstone: [[love|Love]], [[fear|Fear]], [[apology|Apology]], [[gratitude|Gratitude]]. Feelings, expressions, and first-person self-narratives ("I love," "I fear") are the delivery and access layers through which such a binding presents. They do not define it, and swapping them out leaves the binding intact.

## Type and formal status

**E (epistemic).** This is a **Mixed** mark ([[two-mark-system|The Two-Mark System]]); the two components carry different tiers and are stated separately. The *restatement* — `B(p) = (p, e_p)` and the four cornerstone (actuator, error) pairs — is **Derived, authority-canonical** ([[authority-canonical-tier]]): the canon fixes these bindings and their assignment, so the only internal move against the restated pairs is to contest the canon that fixes them, not to re-derive them here. The page's own *synthesis* — that "emotion is a regulated binding" is the correct bridge between the general model and the four cornerstones, and that this carving cleanly individuates them — is **Derived, CV** ([[carving-tier]]): a contestable carving, defeasible by a better carving or a counter-instance. As a treatise-side extension the synthesis layer is held **contestable**; it is not itself canonical, and nothing on this page introduces a forced, exempt, or foundational tier — the four-count remains a carving, not a given.

**A (alethic).** On the [[alethic-axis|alethic axis]], the bindings aspire to *map* felt emotional states accurately. The additive reading of `(p, e_p)`, and the reading that treats the felt tone as the emotion's substance, are held to be **inaccurate**, not merely disallowed by convention.

## What it regulates

The page regulates the **[[cardinal-error|cardinal error]]** — calling an emotion a primitive — by making the primitive `p` visibly a *member* of the binding, never the binding itself. It regulates the **feeling-as-definition drift**, in which the reportable feel, the expression, or the ownership narrative is mistaken for the object; those belong to [[qualia-as-narrative-compression|the narrative-compression layer]] and are held distinct from the regulated operation beneath. And it regulates **free-floating cornerstone names**: it forbids the four labels from meaning anything except by their (actuator, error) structure, so that "love" is not a mood one gestures at but the binding of Toward to a relational gap. In each case what is regulated is a boundary — between binding and primitive, between binding and its access layer, between name and structure.

## What regulates it

[[predicate-binding|Predicate Binding]] supplies the general schema this page instantiates; the restatement cannot outrun it. [[regulated-error-signal|The Regulated Error Signal]] fixes that `e_p` is a controlled discrepancy driven toward a setpoint — **regulation, not a conserved invariant** — which forbids reading the binding as a quantity that is preserved rather than reduced. [[directional-primitives|The Directional Primitives]] fixes the four actuators and their count, so the bridge inherits, and cannot quietly expand, the carving of directions. [[causal-error-mark|The Error Mark With Causal Force]] constrains the binding from the answerability side: an `e_p` that could never produce a change in `p` would be ornamental, and a binding built on an inert error is not a controller at all. The [[fear-guard|fear-guard]] governs where Fear may appear once bound: as an ingredient of [[submission|Submission]] and as the object [[reconciliation|Reconciliation]] reconciles, but never as an ingredient of Reconciliation — this page states Fear as a binding and hands its compositional placement to that guard.

## Valid attack surface

A legitimate attack works at the binding layer, not below it. It must either:

1. **exhibit a cornerstone emotion whose canonical behavior cannot be captured by its actuator and its regulated error** — show that Love does more, or other, than Toward-under-relational-gap can account for, in a way that no refinement of the error term recovers; or
2. **exhibit a second, distinct binding with the same `(p, e_p)` pair but a predictably different function** — two emotions sharing one direction *and* one error yet behaving differently, which would show the pair underdetermines the emotion.

Adjacent legitimate moves: propose a fifth indispensable cornerstone the four cannot absorb (a counter-instance to the count, resolved at [[carving-tier]]), or show two of the four collapse to one pair (a better carving). What is **not** a valid attack is a substrate, expression, or self-report difference offered against the binding — a token of feeling brought against a type of control. That is a level mismatch, and it is answered by pointing to the access layer, not by revising the pair.

## What happens if isolated

The bridge fails in a distinct, testable way at each end.

Hold the general schema `B(p) = (p, e_p)` **without** the four instantiations and it becomes an empty form: a well-typed slot that regulates nothing, because no actual actuator is bound to any actual error. It can classify anything and therefore individuates nothing.

Hold the four cornerstones **without** the general schema and they decay to four names — a mnemonic list whose members are told apart only by their ordinary meanings, which is precisely the [[cardinal-error|cardinal error]] creeping back in through the labels. The four become primitives again by default.

Because the two isolation failures are different (empty schema versus free-floating labels), the coupling of schema-to-instance is not decorative: each end supplies exactly what the other's isolation destroys.

## What larger property emerges from the coupling

At the binding layer, the emergent property *is the emotion*: a regulated behavior present in neither the bare actuator nor the bare error, arising only when the direction is wired to the discrepancy it reduces — the same shape of emergence that [[force|Force]] exhibits one level up, where two bindings coupled by `⊕` yield a motive structure in neither.

At the page layer, coupling the general model to the four instances yields **name-free, substrate-portable individuation**: the four emotions become identifiable by their control structure — which direction, which regulated error — rather than by their felt tone or their words. A system that never uses "love" or "fear" can still be found to contain the binding of Toward to a relational gap. This is what lets the emotions be recognized across carriers (see [[qualia-as-narrative-compression|Qualia as Narrative Compression]] for the access-layer side) and what makes them available as the composable inputs `⊕` requires. Individuation-by-structure is the property the bridge exists to produce.

## What would actually kill the claim

The claim dies if **the model cannot distinguish the four emotions except by importing their ordinary names** — that is, strip the words "love," "fear," "apology," "gratitude," and if the four `(p, e_p)` pairs no longer separate the four functions, the bridge has no content beyond relabeling. Concretely, the claim is killed by either horn of the valid attack landing: a cornerstone whose canonical behavior its pair provably cannot capture, or two distinct emotions forced onto one shared pair. It is also killed if the pairing turns out to be doing no work — if the same functional carving is reachable without the actuator/error decomposition at all.

A residue that does **not** kill it: the felt qualities of the four differ, obviously, and someone may insist the difference in feel is the real difference. That is answered, not defeated — the feel is the delivery of the binding, and the standing counter-instance that *would* defeat the claim is a difference in feel with **no** accompanying difference in direction, error, or regulated consequence. That specific counter-instance has not been exhibited here; producing it is the open work, and its absence is not counted as confirmation.

## Prohibited misreadings

- **`B(p) = (p, e_p)` as addition, or as feeling-plus-cause.** The object is an ordered binding. The `+` in the four restated lines is shorthand for the pairing only; it is never arithmetic and never the `⊕` of [[force|Force]].
- **The emotion *is* the primitive `p`.** This is the [[cardinal-error|cardinal error]] in its purest form. The primitive is a member of the binding, not the binding.
- **`e_p` as a conserved invariant.** The error is regulated *toward a setpoint*, reduced, not preserved. Reading it as a quantity the system holds constant inverts the control relation.
- **The feeling, the expression, or the "I love / I fear" narrative as the definition.** These are the delivery and access layers ([[qualia-as-narrative-compression|Qualia as Narrative Compression]]); the binding is defined without them and survives their variation.
- **Fear here made available to Reconciliation.** This page states Fear as a binding; the [[fear-guard|fear-guard]] alone governs its placement, and Fear is never an ingredient of [[reconciliation|Reconciliation]].
- **Promoting the restatement or the four-count to a forced or exempt tier.** The restated pairs inherit [[authority-canonical-tier|AC]]; the synthesis is [[carving-tier|CV]]; neither becomes founded, and the count of four remains a contestable carving, not a given.
