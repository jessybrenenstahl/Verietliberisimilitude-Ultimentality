# System Invariants

## Definition

A **system invariant** is a relation whose removal has been shown to destroy a function the framework regulates — the load-bearing residue that any rewrite must still enforce, as distinct from any single wording of it. This page is the canonical implementation checklist: the short list of relations that must survive every rewrite, every recompression, and every change to the marking apparatus itself.

The two halves of the framework's central claim fix the two ends of the list. *Nothing stands outside meaning* becomes the access invariants (no extra-symbolic access, no possession, all claims Derived). *Nothing meaningful stands outside correction* becomes the correction invariants (marks with causal force, answerable continuation, refusal that never becomes consent). Between them sits the one coupling that generates the rest — [[semantic-closure-and-recursive-marking|`U = C ⊕ M`]] — whose emergent property, [[no-escape-no-exemption|no escape and no exemption]], is the reason a checklist like this can exist without becoming a creed.

An invariant is therefore closer to a *condition* than to a *sentence*. In the vocabulary of [[frame-condition-and-formulation|frame condition and formulation]], each row names a `C`; the rows are stated in words, and every such wording is a `c_i` that may be deleted, split, narrowed, or replaced. The list catalogues what a valid successor formulation must keep doing, not the exact strings it must keep saying.

## Type and formal status

**E (epistemic).** Exposition, held **treatise-side and contestable**. This page adds no new load-bearing claim; it indexes relations argued and marked on their own pages, so its own mark is Exposition (Derived). Each row **inherits the tier of its source pages** — some **FT** (follows from accepted definitions inside the frame), some **CV** (a carving contestable by counter-instance or a better carving), some **AC** (an authority fixes the operator or binding semantics). The enumeration itself — *which* relations count as invariant, and the framing "must survive every rewrite" — is the contestable, treatise-side part, and is not authority-canonical.

**A (alethic).** The accuracy aspiration is exactness of the carving: the checklist should list every relation whose loss is fatal and no relation whose loss is survivable. Over-inclusion (a decorative row) and under-inclusion (a missing dependency) are both **mapping-accuracy failures**, not mere labeling preferences. Note the standing orthogonality: this page being Exposition on the E axis says nothing about its accuracy on the A axis — see invariant 4.

## The record schema

Each invariant is one record with the same fields, so a build or an auditor can read it uniformly.

```text
record fields per invariant:
  invariant · formal statement · source pages · type
  valid attack · regulator · isolation failure
  kill condition · required tests
```

## The invariants

**1 — No extra-symbolic participant-access.**
*Formal statement:* all participant-access is closed under symbolic transformation; there is no access outside structured signification.
*Source:* [[formal-closure-claim]] · [[axiom]] · [[no-escape-no-exemption]] · [[externality-without-extra-symbolic-access]].
*Type:* Derived, FT (frame condition).
*Valid attack:* exhibit intelligible participant-access that is neither structured signification nor exposed to possible correction, or show the access criterion is overinclusive.
*Regulator:* recursive marking, which keeps closure from hardening into an exhaustive account.
*Isolation failure:* closure without marking becomes totalization.
*Kill condition:* a coherent participant-access relation whose intelligibility and causal role do not instantiate structured signification.
*Required tests:* a same-level access counter-instance; a check that the criterion excludes arbitrary causation ([[category-error-atlas]]).

**2 — No possession of the mapped case.**
*Formal statement:* access maps without possessing; the map is never the mapped territory (accurate participation without possession).
*Source:* [[vls]] · [[semblance]] · [[externality-without-extra-symbolic-access]].
*Type:* Derived, FT (frame-internal).
*Valid attack:* show an access that literally produces or contains its object while still functioning as access.
*Regulator:* returned external consequence and the [[alethic-axis|accuracy aspiration]] — the map answers to what it does not own.
*Isolation failure:* a possession claim collapses map into territory and forfeits correction by externality.
*Kill condition:* exhibit possession of the mapped case that remains participant-access.
*Required tests:* the source-versus-format independence check ([[causal-source-and-experiential-format]]).

**3 — All content-bearing claims are Derived.**
*Formal statement:* every content-bearing claim carries the Derived mark; none is Forced, founded, exempt, or unmarked — including claims about closure and marking.
*Source:* [[derived]] · [[two-mark-system]] · [[frame-condition-and-formulation]].
*Type:* Derived, FT (methodological).
*Valid attack:* exhibit a content-bearing claim that must be exempt from marking in order to function, and cannot be restated as Derived.
*Regulator:* recursive marking under [[self-application]] — the apparatus marks its own statements.
*Isolation failure:* any unmarked tier is the [[textual-nephilim|Textual Nephilim]] — a formulation absolutized as founded.
*Kill condition:* a claim whose function requires exemption from marking with no Derived restatement.
*Required tests:* run the marking apparatus on the apparatus's own statements; confirm no row here is treated as exempt.

**4 — Epistemic exposure and alethic accuracy remain orthogonal.**
*Formal statement:* the E axis (contestability tier FT/CV/AC) and the A axis (mapping accuracy) never predict each other; **contestable never means probably wrong**, and accurate never means beyond contest.
*Source:* [[two-axis-ledger]] · [[alethic-axis]] · [[two-mark-system]].
*Type:* Derived, FT.
*Valid attack:* produce a systematic case where a claim's tier fixes its accuracy, or its accuracy fixes its tier.
*Regulator:* the two-mark system's insistence on two independent marks.
*Isolation failure:* collapse to one axis — contestability read as probable-wrongness, or accuracy read as a licence from contest.
*Kill condition:* exhibit a reliable mapping from position on one axis to position on the other.
*Required tests:* cross-tabulate marks; confirm high-CV claims can be highly accurate and AC claims can be inaccurate.

**5 — Primitive, binding, derivative, composite, role, and application layers remain type-distinct.**
*Formal statement:* the layer stack is type-stratified; a claim at one layer is not answered by a claim at another.
*Source:* [[predicate-binding]] · [[directional-primitives]] · [[conceptual-derivatives]] · [[same-level-attack-rule]].
*Type:* Derived, CV — the layering is a carving; its count is contestable.
*Valid attack:* a same-level counter-instance showing two layers are indistinguishable, or a missing indispensable layer.
*Regulator:* the [[same-level-attack-rule|same-level attack rule]] and [[attack-type-matching|attack-type matching]].
*Isolation failure:* layer collapse produces category errors — primitive/binding inversion, process offered against operator, substrate offered against function.
*Kill condition:* two layers collapse with no behavioral difference, or the stratification blocks no invalid move.
*Required tests:* the [[category-error-atlas]] regression phrases must still be rejected for a stated type reason.

**6 — `B(p) = (p,e_p)` means regulation, not invariant preservation.**
*Formal statement:* a binding is a primitive actuator regulated toward a setpoint by the error it reduces — not a conserved quantity, and not a primitive in its own right.
*Source:* [[predicate-binding]] · [[emotion-as-regulated-binding]] · [[regulated-error-signal]].
*Type:* Derived, AC for the binding model; Derived, CV for the count of four cornerstones.
*Valid attack:* a cornerstone binding whose canonical behavior is not captured by its actuator and regulated error, or a second binding with the same pair but predictably different function.
*Regulator:* the [[causal-error-mark]] requirement — the error signal must be able to change `p`.
*Isolation failure:* read as a primitive it is the [[cardinal-error]]; read as a conserved invariant it stops regulating.
*Kill condition:* the model cannot distinguish the emotions except by importing their ordinary names.
*Required tests:* substitute actuator or error and check the predicted binding behavior.

**7 — `⊕` means coupled-controller dynamics, not addition.**
*Formal statement:* a Force is coupled-controller composition of two bindings; `⊕` is never `+`, sum, synthesis, or blend.
*Source:* [[force]] · [[submission]] · [[reconciliation]] · [[opposed-gradient-contention]] · [[sequential-gating]].
*Type:* Derived, AC (canonical operator semantics).
*Valid attack:* a removal test, or a demonstration that the composite behavior actually lives in one part rather than in the coupling.
*Regulator:* the removal test formalized in [[cross-regulated-necessity]], plus the structural signatures of [[opposed-gradient-contention|opposed-gradient contention]] and [[sequential-gating|sequential gating]].
*Isolation failure:* the additive reading makes a composite an arithmetic sum and loses the emergent coupling.
*Kill condition:* exhibit the emergent property present in an isolated term without the coupling.
*Required tests:* remove one controller and confirm the emergent property vanishes.

**8 — SPLCW returns through a changed world.**
*Formal statement:* Warden → Captive → Logician → Poet → Sculptor → changed world → Warden; the ring is maximal at five and is never declared closed; answerability comes from the witness outside it.
*Source:* [[splcw]] · [[the-ring]] · [[witness-outside-the-ring]] · [[matter-meaning-cycle]].
*Type:* Derived, CV (role topology; the count of five is a carving).
*Valid attack:* a missing function, an invalid dependency, a genuine irreducible sixth role, or a completed loop with no world-change.
*Regulator:* the [[witness-outside-the-ring|witness outside the ring]] and [[free-wont|free won't]], which keep answerability off the ring's own seats.
*Isolation failure:* declaring the ring closed or seizing the witness seat is the [[nephilim|Nephilim]]; a loop with no return is [[sterility]].
*Kill condition:* SPLCW completes without world-change, or a sixth indispensable function is required.
*Required tests:* trace one cycle end-to-end for the return edge; confirm no role occupies the witness seat.

**9 — Continuation requires answerability.**
*Formal statement:* symbolic continuation stays within the Telos only where propagation remains answerable; raw outlasting is not the Telos, and the answerability predicate is never dropped.
*Source:* [[telos]] · [[symbolic-immortality]] · [[answerability-predicate]] · [[continuation-and-colonization]] · [[responsible-successor]].
*Type:* Derived, FT.
*Valid attack:* exhibit valued continuation with the answerability predicate removed, or a successor fully compelled yet still meaningfully answerable.
*Regulator:* the [[responsible-successor|responsible-successor]] conditions and the [[capture-of-corrective-layer|capture]] tests.
*Isolation failure:* continuation without answerability is colonization; answerability with nothing continued carries no structure.
*Kill condition:* a continuation clearly within the Telos whose successors cannot preserve contradiction, revise inheritance, or refuse forced identity.
*Required tests:* the [[continuation-and-colonization]] comparison dimensions applied to a concrete lineage.

**10 — Closure and recursive marking cross-regulate.**
*Formal statement:* `U = C ⊕ M`; closure keeps marking inside meaning, marking keeps every formulation of closure mortal, and each term bounds the characteristic excess of the other.
*Source:* [[semantic-closure-and-recursive-marking]] · [[no-escape-no-exemption]] · [[reciprocal-attack-surfaces]] · [[cross-regulated-necessity]].
*Type:* Derived, FT for the relation; Derived, CV for the notation.
*Valid attack:* exhibit a third irreducible regulator required to close a gap the pair cannot regulate, or show that one principle's attack surface is not in practice occupied by the other.
*Regulator:* each term is the other's regulator — closure ↔ marking.
*Isolation failure:* closure alone totalizes; marking alone regresses into paralysis.
*Kill condition:* remove one controller while still preserving no-escape, no-exemption, anti-totalization, and anti-regress.
*Required tests:* the removal test on each controller ([[reciprocal-attack-surfaces]]).

**11 — No error mark counts unless it can have causal force.**
*Formal statement:* a mark, objection, or contradiction is answerable only when `e_p` can produce a change `Δ(p)` — revision, inhibition, deletion, retyping, demotion, or recodification.
*Source:* [[causal-error-mark]] · [[effective-and-ornamental-answerability]] · [[causal-falsifiability]].
*Type:* Derived, FT synthesis.
*Valid attack:* produce a marked claim whose error can never alter any relevant operation yet is called answerable.
*Regulator:* the recording / routing / effect tests of [[effective-and-ornamental-answerability|effective versus ornamental answerability]].
*Isolation failure:* causal force without marking is untracked volatility; marking without causal force is ornamental humility.
*Kill condition:* recorded error is treated as answerability despite a permanently closed update path.
*Required tests:* trace evidence → changed state, plus a [[causal-falsifiability|regression test]] showing the old failure no longer passes.

**12 — Refusal does not become consent.**
*Formal statement:* refusal is participation, but participation is not consent; unavoidable participation cannot be read as agreement, authorization, endorsement, or approval.
*Source:* [[refusal-as-participation]].
*Type:* Derived, FT clarification; Derived, CV in application.
*Valid attack:* a refusal that leaves no significance, consequence, or altered relation yet remains intelligible as refusal, or a demonstration that the distinction cannot be maintained.
*Regulator:* the [[totalization-boundary|totalization boundary]], where conversion of refusal into support is a named totalization indicator.
*Isolation failure:* without the distinction, semantic closure hardens into coercive enclosure — the participatory trace is read as consent.
*Kill condition:* any governing system may redescribe refusal as consent on the ground that both are participatory.
*Required tests:* for each refusal case, log both the participatory trace and the remaining absence of consent.

## Invariant, formulation, and count

The page fails its purpose the moment these three are confused.

- An **invariant** is a relation that must keep operating. It is the `C`-level object of [[frame-condition-and-formulation|frame condition and formulation]].
- A **formulation** is any finite `c_i` — a sentence, formula, or operational definition of that relation. Formulations are mortal: reworded, split, or replaced without abandoning the invariant, provided *some* formulation still blocks the failure the invariant names. A revision that leaves no formulation able to block that failure has abandoned the invariant, not merely restyled it.
- A **count** is a carving — four primitives, two Forces, three corruptions, five roles, six layers. **Every count is CV** and none appears on this list as an invariant. What is invariant is the *relation the count organizes*: not "there are exactly four primitives," but "predicates bind primitive actuators to regulated errors." A better carving may re-number the parts without touching the invariant, and re-numbering is never by itself a refutation of the relation.

## Invariant recognition, not founding

This checklist is the framework's single most tempting site for the [[textual-nephilim|Textual Nephilim]]: a list titled "what survives every rewrite" reads almost irresistibly as a Forced, founded, exempt tier. It is not, and the guard is structural, not merely asserted.

Each row is present by **invariant recognition** in the sense fixed by [[outcomes-of-attack|the outcomes of attack]]: an attack has shown that removing the relation destroys the function it regulates. That is a **survival record under pressure, not an exemption from pressure**. Every row therefore carries a live kill condition, and the row is itself Derived, deletable, revisable, and retypable. The list is **self-verifying, not self-certifying** ([[self-verifying-not-self-certifying]]): denying any row still enacts the medium the row describes, yet no row certifies that its current wording is complete. The page **indexes** the other pages' marks; it does not sit above them and bless them — treating it as the authority that validates the rest would seize exactly the seat the framework forbids.

Because the page can change what it lists, it is genuinely wounded by its own errors. The [[self-sealing-test|self-sealing test]] and [[postfalsifiability|postfalsifiability]] apply to it directly: run them after any change to the marking apparatus, and if a "failed attack" on a row is ever logged as confirmation of the row, that is a defect in this page, not a strength.

## Prohibited misreadings

- **The checklist as a Forced or founded tier.** No row is exempt from marking. Each is Derived and carries a live kill condition; the list is a marked, reopenable index, never a canon standing outside contest. Reading it otherwise is the [[textual-nephilim|Textual Nephilim]].
- **An invariant as a count.** Counts are CV carvings; the invariant is the relation, not the tally. Re-numbering the parts is not a refutation of the relation.
- **"Survives every rewrite" as "can never be reworded."** Formulations (`c_i`) are mortal; the invariant is what a valid rewrite must still make some formulation enforce.
- **A failed attack on a row as confirmation of the row.** A failed attack is logged as a failed attack ([[outcomes-of-attack]], [[postfalsifiability]]); it never upgrades a claim's status.
- **The page as certifier of the others.** It indexes and cross-links; it does not verify from above. Self-verifying, not self-certifying.
- **The table as exhaustive.** Over- and under-inclusion are accuracy failures ([[attack-surface-matrix]]); the list is reopenable, and a missing dependency is a defect to be fixed, not a boundary to be defended.
- **This being Exposition as a claim about its accuracy.** The E and A axes are orthogonal (invariant 4): being an index says nothing about whether the index is right.
