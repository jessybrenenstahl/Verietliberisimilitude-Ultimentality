# The Prohibited Collapses

## Definition

A **prohibited collapse** is the identification of two distinct types as one — a category error the framework has already paid to keep apart. Each entry has the form `X != Y`, where `!=` reads *is not the same type as*: not *is the opposite of*, not *is worse than*. The page is a single lookup index. One row per collapse, each row naming the two types being conflated, the damage the conflation produces, and the **bridge page** that holds the distinction open. The index is keyed on both terms, so a reader who arrives searching either side lands on the same row and is routed to its correction.

This page authors no new prohibition. Every distinction below is load-bearing somewhere else — on the page named in its **Bridge** column — and inherits that page's mark. The index only gathers them, so that a collapse can be caught by lookup rather than rediscovered by comparing distant pages. Where the [[category-error-atlas|The Category-Error Atlas]] organizes errors by their source and target *layers*, this page enumerates the specific type-pairs and their bridges.

## Type and formal status

**E:** Derived, **Exposition**. The page adds no new load-bearing claim; it restates and cross-links distinctions each of which is Derived on its own source page at that page's tier — **FT** where the distinction follows from accepted definitions inside the frame, **CV** where it is a carving contestable by counter-instance. No entry here is promoted to a founded, forced, or exempt status: the collapses are prohibited because they are *inaccurate*, not because a tier forbids them.

**A:** mapping-accuracy aspiration — each row claims that its two sides pick out genuinely different operations in the modelled domain. A row is inaccurate exactly if its two sides collapse to one type under scrutiny.

This is a treatise-side navigation extension, **held contestable**: the set of rows is an open, marked index, never a closed canon of forbidden words, and its grouping into clusters (below) is itself a CV carving.

## How to read a row

`!=` is a boundary between types, read *is not the same operation as*. It ranks neither side and it names no enemy: `access` is not the opposite of `possession`, and `refusal` is not the opposite of `consent` — they are simply different types that must not be identified. Each row is symmetric for lookup; the index is keyed on both terms, so arriving from either side reaches the same correction.

The **Bridge** column is not a citation of authority. It is the page that *keeps the distinction open* — it carries the distinction's own valid attack surface and its own kill condition. To contest a row, contest it there: this index inherits whatever standing the bridge page holds, and no more.

## Where the collapses cluster

The nineteen rows are not a flat list; each protects an invariant the framework has paid to keep. Grouped by that invariant:

- **No escape / access.** `access != possession`, `external != extra-symbolic`, `meaning != language`, `symbolic != propositional` — all guard the claim that nothing stands outside structured signification, while nothing inside it is thereby owned.
- **The two axes.** `contestable != probably wrong`, `accurate != exhaustive`, `semantic closure != doctrinal completion` — keep epistemic exposure and mapping-accuracy from predicting each other, and keep closure from turning into completeness.
- **Binding and composition.** `primitive != predicate binding`, `binding != invariant preservation`, `composite != arithmetic sum`, `role != personality` — protect the derivation order (actuator, bound error, coupled controller, function) against flattening.
- **Answerability.** `recorded criticism != effective correction`, `participation != consent`, `plurality != causal independence`, `persistence != answerable continuation` — keep correction causal and keep continuation answerable.
- **Identity.** `substrate difference != type difference`, `functional identity != equal capacity` — hold type identity apart from both its carrier and its capacity profile.
- **Compression.** `compression != omission`, `self-verification != self-certification` — keep the kernel dependency-preserving and keep enactment of the medium from certifying the medium's own account.

The clusters are a reading aid, not a partition. A row may bear on more than one invariant, and this grouping is Derived, CV — a better carving may regroup it.

## What would actually kill this index

Being Exposition, the index dies exactly where its rows stop marking real distinctions. Exhibit a listed pair that is one type at one abstraction level, so `!=` marks nothing; or exhibit a bridge page that does not in fact regulate the collapse assigned to it; or show that the wiki commits a listed collapse in one of its own load-bearing claims with no page able to correct it. Any of these deletes or revises the row — not the reader who reported it. A failed report is logged as a failed report, never counted as confirmation that the index is complete or that every remaining row is sound. Charges are cross-checked by [[same-level-attack-rule|The Same-Level Attack Rule]] — a collapse is a valid attack only when pressed at the level where the claim is made — and by [[self-sealing-test|The Self-Sealing Test]], which asks whether any row on this page can be deleted at all.

## Prohibited misreadings

- **The list is closed.** It is not. The index is marked and open; rows are added and revised. Treating it as a sealed catalogue of forbidden words is the very totalization failure the `semantic closure != doctrinal completion` row prohibits.
- **`!=` means opposition or condemnation.** It marks a type boundary, not a rivalry and not a taboo. Naming a collapse is not naming a villain; the correction is a routing to the bridge page, not an anathema.
- **Reproducing a collapse commits it.** Quoting `access != possession` to teach the distinction does not perform the collapse. The page displays the forbidden identification precisely in order to keep it visible and correctable.
- **The page adds a new prohibition tier.** It adds none. Every row is Derived and inherited; the index holds no authority its source pages lack, and its own carving of rows into clusters is contestable.
- **A row falls to one loose usage.** Finding a sentence where the two words are used interchangeably in ordinary speech is not a same-level counter-instance. A row falls only to a case where the two sides are genuinely one type at the abstraction level the framework asserts them.
